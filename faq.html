FAQ Section

Question 1: What is cancer?

Cancer is when abnormal cells divide in an uncontrolled way. Some cancers may eventually spread into other tissues. There are more than 200 different types of cancer.

Question 2: What are some common types of cancer?

Skin cancer, brain cancer, prostate cancer, intestinal cancer and thyroid cancer are few common cancer types.

Question 3: What are the causes of cancer?

Cancer is caused by accumulated damage to genes. Such changes may be due to chance or to exposure to a cancer causing substance. The substances that cause cancer are called carcinogens. A carcinogen may be a chemical substance, such as certain molecules in tobacco smoke. The cause of cancer may be environmental agents, viral or genetic factors. The following are cancer risk factors: i) biological or internal factors, such as age, gender, inherited genetic defects and skin type ii) environmental exposure, for instance to radon and UV radiation, and fine particulate matter iii) occupational risk factors, including carcinogens such as many chemicals, radioactive materials and asbestos iv) life-style factors

Question 4: Is cancer fatal?

It isn’t right to say that cancer is fatal. Firstly, it's important to say that not all cancers kill you. Overall, more than 50% of people diagnosed with cancer live for more than 5 years. Some cancers have survival rates of more than 90% . Yes, Some forms of cancer are curable. Some are curable no matter when they are caught and some are only curable if they are caught at an early stage. But cancer is also considered fatal.

Question 5: How to prevent from getting cancer?

4 tips to reduce risk of getting cancer: i) Don’t use of tobacco. ii) Eat a healthy diet. iii) Maintain a healthy weight and be physically active. iv) Get yourself protected from the sun.

Question 6: Is there a cure for cancer?

As for now, there are no cure for cancer. But there are treatments such as radiotherapy, chemotherapy that can reduce and hinder the growth of abnormal cancer cells.
